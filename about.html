About us

An EV includes both a vehicle that can only be powered by an electric motor that draws electricity from a battery (all-electric vehicle) and a vehicle that can be powered by an electric motor that draws electricity from a battery and by an internal combustion engine (plug-in hybrid electric vehicle).

History of EV vehicle

In the early 19th century, inventors and scientists like Michael Faraday and Thomas Davenport conducted experiments with electric propulsion, leading to the development of early electric motors and batteries. Electric cars gained popularity in the early 20th century, especially among urban dwellers. They were considered a clean and quiet alternative to gasoline-powered vehicles.
